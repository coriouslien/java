The keywords final, finally, and the method finalize() (now deprecated) serve entirely different 
purposes related to immutability and control, exception handling cleanup, and garbage collection 
cleanup, respectively. 

final: Used for constants, preventing method overriding, or preventing class inheritance.
finally: A block in exception handling that always executes.
finalize: A garbage collector method, though try-with-resources is generally preferred.

The keywords final, finally, and finalize in Java are often confused due to their similar names, but they serve three completely different and unrelated purposes within the language.

Here is a breakdown of each keyword, their roles, and a complete code example.
